How do I put on muscle?

This is a long and complicated topic, but in essence, it boils down to two concepts:

- Progressive overload

- Proper diet


Progressive Overload


Progressive overload is the concept of constantly making progress. Each workout, you want to be doing more work. Either adding more weight to the bar, or doing more repetitions. You don't have to be adding a lot, it can be as little as adding a tiny amount of weight, or adding a single rep, but as long as you're making consistent progress, you're building muscle.

Proper diet


Proper diet is the other major pillar of muscle growth. When you work out, you're causing micro-tears to your body's muscle fibers, so your body has to repair the damage you've caused. Your recovery is powered by food:

First of all, your body needs to be configured for growth. This means being in a net caloric surplus - eat more food energy than you're burning. If you eat less food energy than you burn, your body metabolizes it's fat cells. Certainly, a desireable outcome (to a certain degree), but if you don't have a higher percentage of body fat, you can't gain muscle in a caloric deficit.

Secondly, you need to be consuming enough protein. Your body directly uses protein to repair the damage caused during working out. It uses this protein to build your muscles back bigger and stronger.

Experts recommend getting at least 1.2-1.7g protein per kilogram, or 0.5-0.8g per pound.
